## Sim / mechanics (doc-silent numbers)

8. ⚖ **Monster contact damage is wax** (the candle is the only resource): rat
   bite −4, wickworm lunge −20, Chandler Beast stomp −40. During Dark Grace,
   damage subtracts remaining grace ticks instead. — keeps "the candle is
   everything"; no separate HP pool. — `src/shared/sim/constants.ts`.
9. ⚖ **Dark Grace interactions:** wax pickup during grace auto-relights (LIT);
   standing in a lit-brazier aura pauses the countdown; exiting via ENTRY during
   grace still works. Death cause: "Taken by the Dark".
10. ⚖ **Cupped radius rounding:** `base >> 1` (4→2, 3→1, 2→1, 1→0). Own tile is
    always visible.
11. ⚖ **Brazier aura radius 3** (integer disc `d² ≤ r²+r`), pauses ALL
    cost-1 burn (move/wait/interact/salt/chalk) but **not** surcharges
    (force door 5, light brazier 30). Lighting at exactly wax 30 is legal →
    instant grace, immediately paused by the fresh aura.
12. ⚖ **Cup toggle costs 1 tick, 0 wax** ("free" read as wax-free, not
    time-free). Snuff = 2 consecutive SNUFF actions (channel; any other action
    cancels); relight = 3 RELIGHT actions, needs wax > 0 AND (Flint in inventory
    OR adjacent lit brazier). Snuff-channel ticks still burn 1 wax; relight
    ticks burn 0.
13. **Invalid actions demote to WAIT** (tick advances, base burn applies,
    REJECTED event) — keeps tick == actions-consumed for checkpoint framing and
    makes probing cost wax.
14. ⚖ **No wax cap** (pickups can exceed 500).
15. **Player starts with:** Flint & Striker (permanent), Salt Pouch ×3 charges,
    Chalk ×10 charges. ⚖ charge counts.
16. ⚖ **Salt**: thrown 1–2 tiles in a cardinal direction, lands on first
    walkable tile, creates a salt-line overlay. Ground monsters (rats,
    surfaced wickworms) will not path across salt; moths (flying) and the Beast
    ignore it. Costs 1 wax (basic interact).
17. ⚖ **Chalk**: marks the current tile (overlay, visible when in FOV or seen);
    per-floor, discarded on descend (persistent cross-run chalk is a Redis
    feature, M2+). Costs 1 wax.
18. **Noise model** (for the sound-hunting Beast): moving onto stone FLOOR = 2,
    MOSS/WEBBING = 1, interact/force = 2, wait/cup/snuff/relight/mark = 0. The
    last noise (position + level) is state; the Beast moves `min(level, 2)`
    steps toward it each tick, 0 when you were silent — this realizes "moves 2
    on stone, 1 on moss, 0 when you stand still" as a deterministic rule.
19. **Monster AI dispositions are client-visible sim data** (flee-light,
    heat-seek, sound-hunt, flame-orbit parameters in constants.ts): AI must run
    in the client sim for responsiveness, and behavior is observable anyway.
    **Interaction outcomes are the secrets** (bump→dies, fire→instant-kill,
    brazier→melt, moth-over-webbing→ignite) and live only in
    `src/server/rules/rules.json`, resolved through the RulesPort. The sim
    receives outcomes as an explicit `RuleTable` input — purity preserved.
20. ⚖ **Spawns** (fixed counts rolled from the `spawn` substream, ≥6 tiles from
    entry): F1: 4–6 rats, 1–2 moths. F2: 3–5 rats, 2–3 wickworms, 2–3 moths.
    F3: 2–4 rats, 2 wickworms, 2 moths, 1 Chandler Beast.
21. ⚖ **Wickworm cycle:** burrowed (invisible) → senses cupped flames ≤12 tiles
    (heat) or any player ≤3 (vibration) → telegraph 1 tick when adjacent (dust
    plume) → lunge (−20 wax) → surfaced 2 ticks (vulnerable) → re-burrow.
22. ⚖ **Vesper Moths:** seek lit (not cupped/snuffed) flames within 8 tiles;
    orbiting moth = adjacent-8 to player; each orbiter −1 effective light
    radius (min 0; wax burn unaffected); cupping sheds them (they scatter).
    Moth killed over WEBBING ignites it (secret rule).
23. ⚖ **Fire:** burns 4 ticks per tile, spreads once per tick to adjacent
    WEBBING (sorted order), instantly kills entities standing in it via the
    rules table, player in fire −15 wax/tick. Webbing appears on floors 2–3.
24. **Floors 1–3 only; floor 3 has no STAIRS_DOWN** (slice boundary), an extra
    waystone, and the Beast. Descend carries over wax/candle/inventory;
    seen/chalk/salt/fire reset per floor. Fixed `daySeed = 20260708` in dev;
    instant restart re-runs the same seed (no daily limit in dev).
25. **Waystone banking is a stub sheet** (04 §4.4 layout, real copy): lists the
    run's discovery events; COMMIT disabled with "the Vault listens… (M4)";
    "Bank nothing, press on" closes. Claims engine is M4 scope.
26. ⚖ **Rat wax-seeking (L1 rule):** when neither fleeing bright light (radius
    ≥3) nor swarming in the dark (radius ≤1), rats drift toward the nearest wax
    pickup within 8 tiles and linger — following a rat finds wax. Rats do not
    consume pickups (else there'd be nothing to find).
27. **Epitaph copy** uses the 04 §7 canonical death causes; slice reaches
    "Taken by the Dark" (grace expiry — all monster damage routes through wax),
    "Undone by their own flame" (killed by fire during grace), and "Melted
    before the Chandler Beast" (beast lands the killing blow).

## Isometric conversion (2026-07-08, per updated docs)

35. **Render-only pivot, proven by the corpus:** the golden replay suite
    passed UNREGENERATED after the iso conversion — the sim/gen never
    changed, per invariant ("sim never learns iso exists").
36. **Projection calibration:** iso.ts derives its origin from the Phaser
    isometric TilemapLayer's own `tileToWorldXY(0,0)` plus a half-tile
    offset, so ground diamonds and billboards share one source of truth.
    ⚖ If diamonds and sprites appear offset by exactly half a diamond in
    play, the `+HALF_W/+HALF_H` constants in `Descent.buildFloor()` are the
    knob (Phaser corner-vs-center convention — needs one visual check).
37. **Fog model in iso:** the fog Graphics darkens GROUND diamonds only;
    tall billboards (walls/doors/props/entities) self-dim — hidden until
    seen, 40% alpha when remembered-but-dark, full when lit. Avoids the
    "flat fog diamond can't cover a 96px wall face" artifact.
38. **Occlusion approximation:** `iso.occludes()` fades wall/door billboards
    that sort in front of the player (x+y greater) within a screen window of
    ±48px horizontally and one wall-height vertically — cheap, contained in
    iso.ts, tunable there. 35% alpha / 120 ms per the decided spec.
39. **Grid references:** columns A–X (west→east grid x), rows 1–24 (north→
    south grid y): "K4" = x 10, y 3. Long-press any tile for the plaque;
    unseen tiles report "unseen dark" (no tile-type leaks through fog).
40. **Placeholder iso art:** canvas-generated diamond tileset strip
    (`iso-ground`, index = TileId) + 64×96 wall/door billboards + upright
    entity billboards with a deliberate right-facing asymmetry so E/W
    `setFlipX` reads. Same texture keys the W4 atlases will replace.

68. Crispness pipeline (operator: "why are things pixelated?"). Root cause
    was threefold. (a) `pixelArt: true` in the game config - correct for
    retro sprites, ruinous here: it forces NEAREST filtering and disables
    antialiasing, so the supersampled painterly masters rendered as
    jaggies. Removed; explicit antialias + `mipmapFilter:
    LINEAR_MIPMAP_LINEAR` so 4x masters minify cleanly at scout zoom.
    (b) The ground strip was downsampled to 1x (64x32 cells) and then
    MAGNIFIED by the camera (scout up to 1.6x, delve 2.6x) - stretched
    pixels. Now authored at GROUND_SCALE=2x (128x64 cells) with the
    TilemapLayer rendered at 0.5 scale: world geometry unchanged, zoomed
    cameras sample real texels. Calibration verified (tileToWorldXY
    applies layer scale). (c) Deferred: the RESIZE canvas is sized in CSS
    pixels, so OS display scaling (125-150% on Windows) upscales the whole
    backbuffer ~1.5x - a mild global softness. Fixing it means a
    DPR-scaled backbuffer + HUD metric multiplication (Hud layout
    constants are in buffer px); logged for the M2 resolution revisit.

    popping through wall billboards. Fixes: (a) depth now rides the same
